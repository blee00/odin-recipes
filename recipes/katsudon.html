Katsudon

Description

Katsudon is a Japanese food consisting of breaded, deep-fried pork cutlet over rice, topped with a partially beaten egg and varying condiments. This dish takes its name from two Japanese words: tonkatsu and donburi, which mean pork cutlet and rice bowl dish, respectively.

Interestingly, katsudon has a modern tradition of being eaten by Japanese students prior to important tests or school entrance exams. This tradition was spurred by the katsu part of katsudon, as it's homophonous with another Japanese word meaning "to win" or "to be victorious". This is a recipe for 4 servings.

Ingredients

  • 2 center-cut boneless pork chops, pounded down to 1 centimeter thick
  • Salt and ground black pepper
  • All-purpose flour
  • 5 large eggs, beaten, divided
  • 1 cup panko
  • Oil, for frying
  • 1 1/4 cups dashi soup stock
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups steamed white rice

Steps

  1. Season the pounded pork chops with salt and pepper.
  2. Dust with a light, even coating of flour.
  3. In one shallow bowl, beat 1 of the eggs. Put the panko into another shallow bowl.
  4. Add a thin, even layer of oil to a cast-iron pan or skillet over medium heat. The oil is ready when you drop a panko breadcrumb into it and it sizzles.
  5. Dip the flour-dusted pork into the egg to coat both sides.
  6. Transfer the pork to the panko and press it evenly into the meat to get a good coating.
  7. Carefully lay the pork chops in the hot oil and cook for 5 to 6 minutes on one side, until golden brown.
  8. Flip and cook the other side for another 5 to 6 minutes, or until browned, crispy, and cooked through.
  9. Drain on a plate lined with a paper towel.
  10. Slice your tonkatsu into pieces.
  11. Put the dashi soup stock in a pan and heat on medium heat.
  12. To cook 1 serving of katsudon, put 1/4 of the soup and 1/4 of the sliced onion in a small skillet. Simmer for a few minutes on medium heat.
  13. Add 1 serving of tonkatsu pieces (half of 1 pork cutlet) to the pan and simmer on low heat for a few minutes.
  14. Beat another one of the eggs in a bowl. Bring the soup to a boil and pour the egg over the tonkatsu and onion.
  15. Turn the heat down to low and cover with a lid. Cook until the egg has set and remove it from the heat. The egg should be cooked through.
  16. Serve by placing 1 serving of steamed rice in a large rice bowl. Top with the simmered tonkatsu on top of the rice. Repeat to make 3 more servings.
